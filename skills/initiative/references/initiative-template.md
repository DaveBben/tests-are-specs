# Initiative Template

Copy-paste starting point for the initiative document.

```markdown
Initiative: [Short descriptive title]
Owner: [Who is accountable for this investment]
Status: Proposed
Timeframe: [Planning horizon and rough effort estimate — e.g., Q3 2026 (estimated 4–6 weeks of solo effort)]

## Problem Statement

[What's happening, who it affects, and why it matters. Write this as a
narrative — describe the pain in human terms, ground it in evidence or
honest assumptions, and quantify the impact where possible. If evidence
is thin, name what is assumed vs. observed directly in the prose.]

## Strategic Alignment

[What goal, strategy, or personal objective does this support? Name it
explicitly. For personal/solo projects, this can be a personal productivity
goal or learning objective — it doesn't need to be a corporate OKR. Then
explain why now — what external change (technology, market, behavior,
regulatory) makes this the right time?]

## Proposed Solution

[High-level direction — a paragraph, not a spec. Enough to convey the
approach, key technology choices, and how the user will experience it.
Explicitly open to change as design and implementation progress.]

## Alternatives Considered

[What other approaches were evaluated and why were they rejected? Include
"do nothing / status quo" as one of the alternatives. Write each as a
named alternative with 1-2 sentences explaining why it was rejected.]

## Prioritization Rationale

[Why this initiative over other things competing for the same time and
energy. What makes this the right investment right now? Reference the
alternatives and explain the deliberate choice.]

## Success Metrics

[Specific, measurable outcomes that define success. Each metric should
have a target number or threshold and be verifiable. Bullet list format.]

## Guardrail Metrics

[Metrics that must NOT get worse as a result of this initiative. These
protect adjacent concerns while you optimize for the success metrics.
Bullet list format.]

## Scope

**In scope:** [What this initiative explicitly covers. Specific enough
that two people would agree on whether a given capability is in or out.
Comma-separated or bullet list.]

**Out of scope (for now):** [What this initiative does NOT cover. At least
2-3 specific exclusions. Name what someone might assume is included but
is not, and briefly note when/if it might be revisited.]

## Risks and Dependencies

[What could go wrong or block progress. Include both internal risks
(assumptions that could be wrong, technical complexity, time estimates)
and external risks (API stability, platform dependencies, third-party
reliability). Write each risk as a short paragraph with the risk name,
why it matters, and how you'd adapt if it materializes.]

## Milestones

[Meaningful delivery checkpoints that show incremental progress. Each
milestone should be independently testable or demonstrable. Write as
named milestones with a short description of what "done" looks like.]
```
